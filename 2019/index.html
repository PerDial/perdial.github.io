Overview

The 1st International Workshop on Pervasive Computing and Dialogue Systems (PerDial 2019) aims to serve as a platform for cross-disciplinary discussions on ways, problems, challenges and perspectives of mutual integration of Dialogue Systems and Pervasive Computing technologies.

The 1st International Workshop on Pervasive Computing and Dialogue Systems (PerDial 2019)
in conjunction with the IEEE International Conference on Pervasive Computing and Communications (PerCom 2019)
11-15, March 2019
Kyoto, Japan

Call for Paper

Dialogue Systems have been a hot topic of discussion in a research community for the last two decades. They are an inalienable part of Human-Computer Interaction, and allow the integration of Artificial Intelligence systems in our everyday lives smoothly and naturally, through an easy, human-like communication. Pervasive Computing technologies can contact the user at a close level as well as be insensibly integrated into the environment, providing spatially distributed information. Being a part of the smart environment each smart device serves as a mini personal assistant and requires an ability to interact with the user to be able to be more adaptive, user-friendly and effective.

While both topics have recently been an interest of researchers, questions of integration between pervasive computing and dialogue systems were discussed separately in these communities and are not studied deeply. The more active cooperation between both topics opens the way to the future Pervasive Computing Systems. PerDial 2019 will help to explore possible challenges and suggestions to progress them. We aim to create the first and unique opportunity for discussion and collaboration between experts from these disciplines.

Topics of this workshop include, but are not limited to:

  • Use of dialogue systems in pervasive computing environment
  • Experimental design of dialogue systems for pervasive computing
  • Integration of dialogue system into smart environment
  • Dialogue management between smart environment and user
  • User-centered smart environment adaptation
  • Perspective future applications of dialogue systems in pervasive computing
  • Analysis of sensing data collected with IoT and/or smart devices for dialogue systems
  • Pervasive sensing platform/framework for dialogue systems
  • Context recognition and application methodology for dialogue systems
  • User studies and evaluation in the wild of pervasive dialogue system
  • Investigation of Human-Agent Interaction (HAI) in smart environment
  • Dialogue systems for many unspecified people in smart environment
